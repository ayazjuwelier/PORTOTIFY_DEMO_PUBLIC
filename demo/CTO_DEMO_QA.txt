Portotify — Demo Q&A (Technical Audience)

Q1 — Is Portotify replacing the AI model or controlling it?

Short answer

No. Portotify does not replace the AI model.

It sits between the user and the AI system as a governance layer.

Explanation

The AI model still generates the response.
Portotify adds an additional layer that:

analyzes the request

classifies decision risk

enforces governance rules

produces governance artifacts

Think of it as a control and accountability layer, not a model replacement.

Q2 — How is the risk classification determined?

Short answer

Risk classification is deterministic and rule-based.

Explanation

Portotify evaluates signals such as:

tool calls

external writes

human review presence

override usage

These signals are combined into a Risk Tier.

The goal is not prediction accuracy but governance transparency.

Risk tiers make the system auditable.

Q3 — What exactly is a Decision Capsule?

Short answer

A Decision Capsule is a governance artifact representing an AI decision.

Explanation

Each capsule records:

decision request

risk classification

reasoning for the classification

execution reference

governance metadata

Capsules make AI decisions traceable and auditable.

They can later be used for:

audits

incident analysis

governance reporting

compliance review

Q4 — Does Portotify block risky decisions?

Short answer

It can, but that is not its only purpose.

Explanation

Portotify supports multiple governance behaviors:

observation

risk classification

blocking

escalation

human review

The default philosophy is: to make decisions visible and auditable first,
while still allowing policy-based blocking when required.

Q5 — Why is this needed if companies already log AI requests?

Short answer

Logs record events.
Governance artifacts record decisions and accountability.

Explanation

Traditional logs show:

requests

responses

timestamps

Portotify records:

decision risk

governance reasoning

policy context

decision artifacts

This transforms raw AI usage into governance-ready decision records.

Q6 — Is this tied to a specific AI provider?

Short answer

No.

Explanation

Portotify is provider-agnostic.

It can operate with:

OpenAI

Anthropic

Google

local models

future providers

The governance layer is independent of the model provider.

Q7 — What problem is Portotify solving?

Short answer

AI systems make decisions, but most organizations cannot explain or audit those decisions.

Explanation

Portotify addresses three gaps:

Visibility
AI decisions are normally invisible.

Risk classification
Decisions are rarely categorized by risk.

Accountability artifacts
There is usually no durable governance record.

Portotify turns AI decisions into traceable governance artifacts.

One Sentence Summary

Portotify adds a governance layer that turns AI decisions into auditable, risk-classified artifacts.
